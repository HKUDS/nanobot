# Judging

## Overview

Judging happens in **two rounds** on Friday, April 24.

---

## Round 1 — Preliminary Judging (1:00–2:30 PM)

Teams are divided into their **5 tracks**. Each track runs presentations **in parallel** across conference rooms in BIF and Wohlers.

### Presentation Format (7 minutes per team)

| Segment | Duration |
|---------|----------|
| Setup | 1 min |
| Slides + live demo | 3 min |
| Q&A with judges | 2 min |
| Transition out | 1 min |

### What to Prepare

- A **short slide deck**: intro slide + workflow/architecture slide
- A **working live demo** of your AI agent in action
- Be ready to explain: what problem you're solving, how the automation works, and what impact it has

### How It Works

- Track presentations run 1:15–2:00 PM (approx. 45 min per track)
- First team goes in; following teams wait outside
- 2–3 judges per track (the project champion for your track will be one)
- Judges select the **top 1 team per track** → 5 finalists total
- Judges convene 2:05–2:30 PM to align scoring
- **Finalists announced at ~2:40 PM**

### Tracks & Judge Allocation

| Track | Judges |
|-------|--------|
| Finance & Accounting | 2–3 |
| HR | 2–3 |
| Marketing & Sales | 2–3 |
| Business Technology | 2–3 |
| General Innovation | 2–3 |

**Total Round 1 judges:** 6–9

---

## Round 2 — Finals (3:15–4:00 PM)

**Location:** BIF 1001 (Deloitte Auditorium)

The top 5 finalist teams present to a **combined panel of industry judges**.

### Final Presentation Format

| Segment | Duration |
|---------|----------|
| Presentation + live demo | 5 min |
| Q&A | 3 min |

- All participants attend the final presentations
- Panel of 5 corporate/industry judges (plus others from Round 1 if needed)

---

## Judging Criteria

Judges will use a **rubric** that will be shared with students. Detailed feedback will be provided to all teams **after the event**.

Key evaluation areas (general guidance):
- **Problem significance** — Is this a real, meaningful business problem?
- **Automation depth** — Does the agent handle a multi-step workflow, not just one task?
- **Working demo** — Does it actually work live?
- **Business impact** — How much time/money/effort would this save?
- **Innovation** — Is the approach creative or novel?
- **Presentation quality** — Clear, concise, well-structured
