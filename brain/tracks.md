# Focus Area Tracks

Every team must pick one of the five tracks below. Gies business units provide real challenges for each track, and the project champion for your track will also be one of your judges.

---

## 1. Finance & Accounting

**Challenge areas:**
- Invoice processing automation
- Expense management workflows
- Financial reporting and reconciliation
- Budget tracking and alerts

**What judges look for:** Accuracy, auditability, time savings, integration with financial systems.

---

## 2. HR (Human Resources)

**Challenge areas:**
- Candidate screening and resume filtering
- Employee onboarding workflows
- Internal employee inquiry routing
- Policy compliance checks

**What judges look for:** Fairness, efficiency, employee experience impact, scalability.

---

## 3. Marketing & Sales

**Challenge areas:**
- Content generation pipelines
- Campaign management automation
- Marketing analytics and reporting
- Lead qualification and routing

**What judges look for:** Creativity, brand consistency, measurable impact, integration with marketing tools.

---

## 4. Business Technology / Operations

**Challenge areas:**
- Inventory management
- Vendor communication automation
- Supply chain workflow optimization
- IT service request routing

**What judges look for:** Reliability, integration with existing systems, operational efficiency gains.

---

## 5. General Innovation

**Challenge areas:**
- Customer operations (ticket routing, response automation)
- Cross-functional workflow automation
- Any creative business automation that doesn't fit the above tracks

**What judges look for:** Creativity, real-world applicability, potential business impact, novelty.

---

## Choosing Your Track

- Pick the track that best fits your idea during team formation (5:00–5:45 PM, Day 1)
- If unsure, talk to a mentor — they can help you find the right fit
- The project champion for your track is your primary business stakeholder during the event
