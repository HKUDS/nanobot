## Rules & Format

### Teams
- 2–4 members, Gies undergrad/grad students only
- Register during networking session (5:00–5:45 PM, Day 1)

### What to Build
Build an **AI agent that automates a real-world business workflow** using no-code/low-code tools (e.g., Microsoft Copilot Studio).

### What Counts
- AI agents handling end-to-end business processes
- Multi-step workflows with decision logic
- Solutions integrating with real business tools

### What Does NOT Count
- Simple chatbots (Q&A bots)
- Single-prompt solutions
- Manual processes with AI bolted on

### Submissions
- Due: 1:00 PM, Friday April 24
- Need: short slide deck (intro + workflow) and working live demo

## Tracks

Every team picks one track:

1. **Finance & Accounting** — invoice processing, expense management, financial reporting
2. **HR** — candidate screening, onboarding, employee inquiries
3. **Marketing & Sales** — content generation, campaign management, analytics
4. **Business Technology / Operations** — inventory management, vendor communications, supply chain
5. **General Innovation** — customer operations, ticket routing, or anything creative

## Judging

### Round 1 — Preliminary (1:00–2:30 PM)
- Teams present in their track (parallel sessions across rooms)
- 7 minutes per team: 1 min setup, 3 min slides+demo, 2 min Q&A, 1 min transition
- 2–3 judges per track (project champion is one)
- Top 1 team per track advances → 5 finalists
- Finalists announced ~2:40 PM

### Round 2 — Finals (3:15–4:00 PM, Deloitte)
- 5 finalists present to combined panel of industry judges
- 5 min presentation+demo, 3 min Q&A
- All participants attend

### What Judges Look For
- Problem significance
- Automation depth (multi-step, not single task)
- Working live demo
- Business impact
- Innovation/creativity
- Presentation quality

Judges use a rubric. Feedback shared with all teams after the event.
