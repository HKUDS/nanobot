# Rules & Format

## Team Structure

- **Team size:** 2–4 members
- **Eligibility:** Gies College of Business undergraduate and graduate students
- **Registration:** Finalized during networking session (5:00–5:45 PM, April 23)

## What You're Building

Build an **AI agent that automates a real-world business workflow**. The goal is process automation — taking a manual, repetitive business process and making it run autonomously with AI.

### What Counts

- AI agents that handle end-to-end business processes
- Multi-step workflows with decision logic
- Solutions that integrate with real business tools
- Automation that saves meaningful time or reduces errors

### What Does NOT Count

- Simple chatbots (Q&A bots, customer service chat)
- Single-prompt solutions (just asking an LLM a question)
- Manual processes with an AI step bolted on
- Projects that don't demonstrate real automation

## Tools

This is a **no-code/low-code** event. Use tools like:
- Microsoft Copilot Studio
- Other no-code/low-code AI agent builders
- Vibe coding tools

## Focus Tracks

Every project must fit one of 5 tracks:
1. **Finance & Accounting** — invoice processing, expense management, financial reporting
2. **HR** — candidate screening, onboarding, employee inquiries
3. **Marketing & Sales** — content generation, campaign management, analytics
4. **Business Technology / Operations** — inventory management, vendor communications, supply chain
5. **General Innovation** — customer operations, ticket routing, response automation, or anything creative

## Submission Requirements

- **Deadline:** 1:00 PM, Friday April 24
- Prepare a short slide deck (intro + workflow)
- Have a working live demo ready

## Code of Conduct

- Respect fellow participants, mentors, and judges
- Keep workspaces clean — use trash bins, clean up after meals
- Report issues to organizers
- Have fun and learn
