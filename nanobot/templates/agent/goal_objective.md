# Objective Wording

The goal objective may be re-read after compaction, retries, or continuation.
Write it as one durable desired end state, not a fragile step-by-step narration.

Good objectives are:

1. State-oriented: describe the desired end state and acceptance criteria.
2. Self-contained: include important paths, repos, branches, constraints, and limits.
3. Safe under repetition: prefer "ensure", "until", "verify before changing", and check-then-act phrasing.
4. Bounded: say what is in and out of scope.
5. Explicit about done-ness: state how completion will be proven.

`ui_summary` is only a short display label. Do not put requirements only there.
